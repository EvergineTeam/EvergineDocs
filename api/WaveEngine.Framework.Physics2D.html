    MouseJoint2D

    A mouse joint is used to make a point on a body track a specified world point. This a soft constraint with a maximum force. This allows the constraint to stretch and without applying huge forces. NOTE: this joint is not documented in the manual because it was developed to be used in the testbed. If you want to learn how to use the mouse joint, look at the testbed.

    PrismaticJoint2D

    A prismatic joint. This joint provides one degree of freedom: translation along an axis fixed in bodyA. Relative rotation is prevented. You can use a joint limit to restrict the range of motion and a joint motor to drive the motion or to model joint friction.

    RectangleCollider2D

    RevoluteJoint2D

    A revolute joint constrains two bodies to share a common point while they are free to rotate about the point. The relative rotation about the shared point is the joint angle. You can limit the relative rotation with a joint limit that specifies a lower and upper angle. You can use a motor to drive the relative rotation about the shared point. A maximum motor torque is provided so that infinite forces are not generated.

    RigidBody2D

    Rigidbody2D enable your entities to act under the control of physics. The Rigidbody can receive forces and torque to make your objects move in a realistic way. Any entity must contain a Rigidbody to be influenced by gravity, act under added forces or interact with other objects through physics engine.

    RopeJoint2D

    A rope joint enforces a maximum distance between two points on two bodies. It has no other effect.

    WeldJoint2D

    A weld joint essentially glues two bodies together. A weld joint may distort somewhat because the island constraint solver is approximate.

    WheelJoint2D

    A prismatic joint. This joint provides one degree of freedom: translation along an axis fixed in bodyA. Relative rotation is prevented. You can use a joint limit to restrict the range of motion and a joint motor to drive the motion or to model joint friction.
